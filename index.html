50° is near-optimal for Redmond winters. Winter sun at 18.9° altitude hits a 50° slope at ~69° incidence—much better than a shallow slope. The steep angle also sheds rain and snow aggressively. Grade slopes toward the north wall, draining into the rubble trench foundation.

Floor Plan

Each 12′×12′ module is entirely under the slope: 8′ glass run + 4′ insulated roof = full 12′ N-S depth. North thermal wall at the back. Water barrels along north wall. Direct in-ground growing on mounds (no raised beds). Grade slopes gently north for drainage to rubble trench. Center wall between modules is glass with a 2-pane window/door opening.

Phase 1 (East)

Standalone 12′×12′. Best glass on south slope. Patio door on east end. West end = pre-framed for Phase 2 center glass wall connection.

Phase 2 (West)

Joins at center. Screen door on west end. Center wall = full glass with 2-pane window/door opening between greenhouses.
